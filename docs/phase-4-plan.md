# Phase 4 plan — the monster editor

Implementation plan for phase 4 of [the osr-editor spec](spec.md). Phase 4 delivers the native monster editor: full stat-block authoring over `MonsterTemplate` — create from scratch and clone-and-modify any catalog monster — bundled-template management on `Adventure.monsters`, and the id-collision rule as an op-level rejection with a rename prompt. Milestone: a bespoke monster authored in the editor is encountered and fought in osr-web.

Phase 4 lands after phase 5, completing the skip that plan recorded: the forge-side monster surfaces (the printed-notation `StatBlockOverride` form, the monster-resolution panel) shipped there and are not this phase's work, and the boundary phase 5 pinned — "when its ops land they will simply join the forge-mode blocked list per this plan's general rule" — is honored here verbatim. Everything phase 4 adds is native-mode authoring; the reproducible-correction world is untouched except for three new entries in its blocked-op table.

Six research facts shape this plan more than any others. First, **the collision rule is osrlib's; the op-level rejection is the editor's posture, and it must not lock foreign documents**: `validate_adventure` emits `bundled monster id {id!r} collides with the catalog` for every shipped-catalog *and* bundle-internal duplicate (`osrlib/crawl/adventure.py:149-151`, via `_effective_monsters`'s first-occurrence-wins union at `adventure.py:93-112`), and the editor's diagnostics tier already maps it (`diagnostics.py:157,171-174`, code `bundled_monster_collision`). The spec pins eager rejection — "a collision is never intentional... the editor refuses it at commit instead of letting it linger as a diagnostic" — which is the `AddDungeon`/`AddFeature` duplicate-id posture; but the check applies to **new or changed ids only**, the exact carry-through rule phase 3's `SetFeature` pinned for out-of-bounds cells: an unchanged colliding id in a foreign document passes through untouched, so its other fields stay editable and the finding stays a navigable diagnostic. The invariant is "no op ever *introduces* a collision," not "no colliding document may be edited." Second, **`MonsterTemplate`'s own validators do most of the enforcement at request parse**: the model couples the AC pair to the auto-hit flag (`attack_roll_required=True` requires both `ac` and `ac_ascending`; `False` forbids both — `osrlib/core/monsters.py:387-394`), hit dice constrain the die to d8 or d4 and demand `fixed_hp` when `count` is 0, attack damage and number-appearing dice must parse under the dice grammar, number-appearing values are dice-XOR-fixed (or `see_below` with neither), and every bound (`thac0` 2–20, `attack_bonus` ≥ −1, `morale` 2–12, saves 2–20, `movement` min 1, `alignment.options` min 1) is a field constraint — so whole-value ops get the entire stat-block shape validated as `request_invalid` before any op logic runs, and the form is shaped valid-by-construction wherever shape can carry the rule (the auto-hit toggle clears or seeds both ACs in one gesture; XORs are radios; dice inputs reuse the phase 3 convenience mirror with the server's parse as authority). Third, **whole-value grain is forced by the model's shape**: 25 fields, six of them tuples of nested models (`AttackRoutine` wrapping ordered `MonsterAttack`s, `MovementMode`, `MonsterAbility` with its typed `params` dict, `AcAlternate`/`MoraleAlternate`/`XpNote` rows) plus the nested `Defenses` cluster — a field-grained op would need a value union undiscriminable on the wire, the phase 3 `SetFeatureField` reasoning an order of magnitude over. The spec's own vocabulary (`AddMonsterTemplate`, `SetMonsterTemplate`, `RemoveMonsterTemplate`) is whole-value, and the card-commit gesture produces the whole model anyway. Fourth, **template renames cascade, by the `RenameDungeon` precedent**: unlike feature ids ("a rename cascades nowhere"), monster template ids are referenced in-document — `KeyedMonster.template_id` on the encounter lines and wandering rows' `monster_ids` resolve against the effective catalog — and `RenameDungeon` (`documents.py:1358-1386`) already pins the answer for referenced re-keys: rewrite every reference in the same commit and answer the honest whole-document delta (`path=""`). Fifth, **in forge-backed projects `Adventure.monsters` is derived state**: assembly bundles exactly the custom templates the built encounters still reference (the phase 5 round-trip equivalence excludes `monsters` for this reason, and `test_overrides.py`'s `normalized()` pops it), so bundled-template authoring is inherently native-only and the three ops join `_BLOCKED_MESSAGES` (`overrides.py:124-134`) and `_blocked_address` (`overrides.py:137-153`) — blocked in place with the detach offer, the spec's own detach-only example ("bundling a monster the module never mentions") made mechanical. Sixth, **the effective catalog stays a client-side merge, but clone needs what the summaries omit**: `catalogs.py`'s module docstring pins "no per-project catalog route exists" and the frontend's `effectiveMonsterCatalog` already ranks bundled templates first — nothing changes there — but `CatalogMonster` is a picker summary, and clone-and-modify needs a shipped monster's **full** stat block, so one new route serves it by id. A related sizing fact: XP, THAC0/attack bonus, and saves are authored fields osrlib can also derive (`monster_xp`/`xp_band_label` at `osrlib/core/tables.py:673-735`, `thac0_for_hd` at `tables.py:591` answering the coupled pair, the monster save bands at `tables.py:613`) — derive-from-HD helpers are deliberately deferred to phase 7, where the authoring-aids pattern (pure preview through a route, never a frontend rules mirror) lives; phase 4 ships the honest form, and clone-and-modify is the spec's own answer to stat-block tedium.

## Scope

In scope:

- `ops.py` growth — `AddMonsterTemplate`, `SetMonsterTemplate`, `RemoveMonsterTemplate`, joined into `AnyEditOp`, with the collision invariant
- `documents.py` growth — op application over `Adventure.monsters`, the rename cascade, the note-remap rule
- The forge blocked-op table growth in `overrides.py`
- `GET /api/catalogs/monsters/{id}` — the full-template detail route clone consumes
- The address grammar's `monster:<id>` segment; `bundled_monster_collision` re-addressed to it
- Frontend — the Monsters nav section (both project types): the template list, the always-saved stat-block editor covering the whole model, create and clone-from-catalog flows with the collision-rename prompt, the confirm-on-referenced remove; the monster picker's create shortcut; the forge-mode offer-in-place behavior and the phase 5 backport it implies (import's new-level mode returns; the resolution panel relabels); the author-notes card on templates
- The small-module fixture grows one bundled bespoke template keyed in an encounter, so the round-trip, golden-compatibility, and publish suites cover bundled monsters permanently
- Type generation updates and the test suites for all of the above, green in CI

Out of scope (each with the phase that picks it up): derive-from-HD conveniences and stat-block previews — XP from the awards table, the THAC0/attack-bonus pair, save-band fills — are authoring aids and land with phase 7's preview-route pattern; the spells and classes catalogs, which phase 3 projected for "phases 4 and 7," turn out to have **no phase 4 consumer** — `MonsterSaves.save_as` is a free printed string and nothing in `MonsterTemplate` references a class or spell id — so both wait for their first real consumer (phase 7's aids, or later), and this plan records the correction; conversion (phase 6); SRD stocking and the prose assistant (phase 7); walk mode (phase 8) — the milestone's fought-in-osr-web half is manual against the sibling checkout, the established pattern; docs site and release engineering (phase 9). The forge-side monster correction surfaces shipped in phase 5 and are out of scope here; the native monster surfaces, by contrast, stay **present in forge mode and block in place** — the spec's own "offers it in place when such an edit is attempted," pinned by owner decision over the absence this plan first drafted: hiding a blocked capability is a discoverability trap that also hides what detach unlocks, and the blocked-op dialog is precisely the just-in-time education the sentence intends. The phase 1 no-dead-surface rule is not violated — these surfaces are live, not disabled; every gesture leads somewhere real (the detach choice) — and disabled-with-tooltip is rejected for the same reason absence was: a disabled control cannot open the dialog that teaches. The one absence phase 5 recorded under the old reasoning (import's new-level mode) is backported to the same posture in item 9.

## Work items

### 1. The ops

New frozen ops in `ops.py`, adventure-scoped (no targeting base — `Adventure.monsters` is top-level, the `SetAdventureField` neighborhood):

- **`AddMonsterTemplate`** — `template: MonsterTemplate`. Appends to `Adventure.monsters` in authored order.
- **`SetMonsterTemplate`** — `template_id: str`, `template: MonsterTemplate`: whole-value replacement, the `SetEncounter`/`SetFeature` grain. A differing `template.id` is a rename, under `AddMonsterTemplate`'s id rejections, cascading per item 2. Among foreign duplicate bundled ids, `SetMonsterTemplate` and `RemoveMonsterTemplate` resolve first-match in authored order — the `SetFeature`/`RemoveFeature` posture.
- **`RemoveMonsterTemplate`** — `template_id: str`. Removal admits dangling references: encounters and wandering rows naming the removed id become `encounter_unknown_monster`/`wandering_unknown_monster` diagnostics — legal while editing, the tier rule; the UI confirms first (item 7).

Invariants, rejected as `OpInvariantError` (`op_invariant`): an empty id; a **new or changed** id that collides with the shipped catalog (`load_monsters()`, cached) or with another bundled template — research fact one's carry-through rule, so `SetMonsterTemplate` keeping a foreign template's already-colliding id passes it through untouched, while `AddMonsterTemplate` (always a new id) and any rename reject the collision outright. An unknown `template_id` is `op_target_not_found`, per the phase 2 rule. Everything else — the AC coupling, dice grammars, XORs, bounds — is the model's own, at request parse (research fact two).

### 2. Application and the cascade

Three `_apply_*` helpers in `documents.py`, rebuilding via `adventure.model_copy(update={"monsters": ...})` with delta pointer `/monsters`. The rename path mirrors `_apply_rename_dungeon`: every `KeyedMonster.template_id` and every wandering-row `monster_ids` entry equal to the old id is rewritten to the new one across all dungeons, levels, and areas in the same commit — one op, one undo step — and the delta is honestly whole-document (`path=""`), since the cascade may touch any area. A rename can only ever retarget references to a *bundled* id (the old id resolves to a bundled template by construction; renaming toward a shipped id is the collision the invariant rejects), and a rename onto an id that dangling references already name simply makes those references resolve — the diagnostic clears, no special handling. `_note_remap_rules` grows a fourth rule: `SetMonsterTemplate` with a differing id remaps the `monster:<old>` note-key prefix to `monster:<new>`, riding the phase 5 cascade-and-undo machinery unchanged.

### 3. Forge blocking

Three entries in `_BLOCKED_MESSAGES` — the shared message "bundled monster templates have no override kind — assembly derives them from the monsters stage" — and a `_blocked_address` branch answering `monster_address(...)` from the op's id (`template.id` for add, `template_id` for set/remove). `ensure_forge_supported` already rejects the whole batch before any side effect; the three ops join the `BLOCKED_OPS` matrix in `test_overrides.py`. Nothing else in the translator changes — the ops can never reach it.

### 4. The catalog detail route

`GET /api/catalogs/monsters/{id}` answers the full `MonsterTemplate` for a shipped id — the clone source. Unknown id → 404 `catalog_monster_not_found` (new typed error, mapped like its neighbors; the remedy names the catalogs route). The route model is osrlib's own `MonsterTemplate` riding the OpenAPI surface — already generated, never mirrored. Bundled templates need no route: the document is in hand client-side, the same reasoning that keeps the effective catalog a client merge. The response serves the template from `load_monsters()` itself — osrlib's cached loader; the list route's summary cache holds `CatalogMonster` picker fields and cannot answer a full stat block.

### 5. Addresses and diagnostics

`addresses.py` grows `monster_address(template_id) -> "monster:<id>"` — a new top-level segment kind, percent-encoded like every id-bearing segment. `bundled_monster_collision` re-addresses from the bare `monsters` scope to `monster:<id>`: the regex grows a capture over osrlib's repr-quoted id, and the extracted id is resolution-checked against `adventure.monsters` (the phase 1 parser's honesty guard — an id that doesn't resolve degrades to the coarser `monsters` scope, which is always true, never a lie). The frontend's `address.ts` replaces its phase 1 placeholder ("Bundled monsters are adventure scope; their own surface is phase 4's"): the `monsters` scope navigates to the Monsters section, and `monster:<id>` navigates to it with that template selected. `encounter_unknown_monster` and `wandering_unknown_monster` keep their area and level addresses — the reference is where the fix usually is.

### 6. Editor-authored conventions and the seed block

Create and clone set `page=""` — the spec's pinned convention, forge's own marker for unpaged blocks — and `overrides_applied=()` (SRD-compiler provenance, meaningless for editor-authored templates). Neither field surfaces in the form, and both carry through untouched on `SetMonsterTemplate` over a foreign template. Create seeds a model-valid stock 1-HD block the form then edits — required fields demand a complete template before the first commit, and the seed is osrlib-derived where derivable, neutral where authored: `ac 9 / ac_ascending 10` (unarmoured), `hit_dice` 1d8, `thac0 19 / attack_bonus 0` (the 1 HD row), one routine of one by-weapon attack named "weapon", movement 120' (40'), saves 12/13/14/15/16 with `save_as "1"` (the 1–3 band), `morale 7`, alignment options `(neutral)`, `xp 10` (1 HD, no asterisks), number appearing 1d6/1d6, empty treasure, abilities, defenses, and categories. Clone fetches the source — the detail route for a shipped monster, the document for a bundled one — and prefills the id with the next-free `<source-id>-<n>` — next-free over the **effective catalog** (shipped plus bundled), the scope the collision invariant checks, so a prefill never lands on a rejection — and the name with the source's, both editable in the dialog before the add commits.

### 7. The Monsters section

A new always-present nav entry in both project types, beside Adventure and Town; the forge-only resolution entry relabels to "Monster resolution" so the two never share a name (item 9). In a forge project the section renders the assembled document's derived bundle — a genuinely useful review view beside the resolution panel's printed-notation one — and every authoring gesture blocks in place per research fact five: flow-entry actions (create, clone, remove) open the blocked-op dialog directly with the would-be op named (client-side routing so no one fills a dialog just to be refused; the server's `op_unsupported_forge` stays the authority for any batch that arrives), and a detail-field commit posts and renders its 422 through the same dialog. The author-notes card stays live in both modes — notes are sidecar annotation, never ops. The pane is list-plus-detail:

- **The list** — every bundled template: name, id and hit dice in monospace, XP, and a referenced-by count computed from the document (encounters plus wandering rows). Header actions: "New monster" (the create dialog: id and name over the seed block) and "Clone catalog monster" (the phase 3 monster picker over the effective catalog, then the clone dialog). Rows select into the detail editor; row removal is two-step confirm (the `CorrectionsPanel` pattern), and a referenced template's confirm states the count and that references become diagnostics, never silently — the remove-area precedent.
- **The detail editor** is always-saved per gesture, the editor's law — no save button, no dirty state: every blur/Enter commits one whole-template `SetMonsterTemplate` batch (one undo step) through builders in a new `lib/monster-builders.ts` mirroring `content-builders.ts`, computing the next template against the committed document at dequeue time. Form sections mirror the model in field order: identity (id — inline collision error via the `commit(..., {onError})` claim on `op_invariant`, the rename prompt the spec names; name; intro in the serif voice); combat — the auto-hit toggle gating the AC pair as a unit (disabling clears both in the same gesture; re-enabling seeds the unarmoured 9/[10]) with `AcAlternate` rows beneath, the hit-dice builder (count, d4/d8 select, signed modifier, asterisks, optional average/fixed hp with the count-0-needs-fixed rule carried by shape), THAC0 and attack bonus in monospace, and attack routines as an ordered list of routines each holding ordered attacks (count, name, damage dice or fixed, by-weapon with modifier, effect tags via `ListEditor`); movement rows (rate, encounter rate, descriptor); the five saves plus save-as; morale and its alternates; alignment (options multi-select, usual constrained to the chosen options); XP and role-note rows; number appearing (dungeon and lair, each a dice/fixed/see-below radio group); the treasure ref (letters, parenthetical, extra gp, multiplier, specials, see-below); abilities (tag, name, prose in serif, the manual flag, and params as key/value rows whose values are JSON-typed monospace inputs — the client mirror validates the fragment, the server's parse is the authority); defenses (harmed-only-by and condition-immunity multi-selects over their enums, reduction rows, the per-element energy map); categories via `ListEditor`. Dice inputs reuse the phase 3 convenience mirror throughout.
- **The author-notes card** — the phase 5 sidecar card on the detail pane, keyed `monster:<id>`, remapped on rename by item 2's rule.

### 8. The picker's create shortcut

The phase 3 `MonsterPicker` grows one footer action — "Create monster…" — navigating into the Monsters section's create flow, so "like an orc, but…" starts where stocking happens. The action is present in forge projects too and routes to the blocked-op dialog (item 7's entry-point rule): the capability is discoverable, and the dialog names detach as what unlocks it. The picker's ranking already surfaces bundled templates first, so a just-authored monster is the top result back in the encounter card — no new ranking work.

### 9. The phase 5 backport

The offer-in-place posture applies retroactively to the one surface phase 5 hid under the old reasoning: the forge-mode import dialog regains its new-level mode as a visible choice that opens the blocked-op dialog (a new level has no override kind; the dialog says so and offers detach), so the capability and its unlock are discoverable from the same place. The forge nav's resolution entry relabels from "Monsters" to "Monster resolution" — descriptive, and it frees the name for the always-present section. The phase 5 plan's recorded "absent, not disabled" exception is amended in place with a superseded-by-phase-4 note (the plan-and-code-never-diverge rule, applied across phases), and phase 5's rule of record — "no forge-mode surface is preemptively hidden" — now holds without exception.

### 10. Type generation

The three ops ride `AnyEditOp` into `OpBatch` — free. The detail route references `MonsterTemplate`, already on the surface. The injection list stays exactly `[ApiError]`; the drift gate is unchanged. `frontend/src/types/index.ts` grows aliases for the nested stat-block models (`AcAlternate`, `AlignmentSpec`, `AttackRoutine`, `MonsterAttack`, `MovementMode`, `MonsterSaves`, `SavingThrows`, `MoraleAlternate`, `XpNote`, `NumberAppearing`, `NumberAppearingValue`, `TreasureRef`, `MonsterAbility`, `Defenses`, `DamageReduction`, `EnergyDefense`, `DamageKey`, `Element`). The vitest type-level suite extends: the union discriminates over the grown vocabulary, `MonsterTemplate.ac` admits `null`, `attacks` nests routine-of-attacks, `NumberAppearingValue.dice` is nullable.

### 11. Tests

Consolidated from the work items:

- **ops** (new `tests/test_monster_ops.py`, on the content-ops scaffold): add/replace/remove; the collision matrix — add vs shipped id, add vs bundled id, rename into either, empty id, and the carry-through (a foreign colliding template's other fields edit cleanly; its unchanged id never rejects); rename cascading encounters and wandering rows with the whole-document delta, and a rename onto a dangling reference resolving it; first-match over foreign duplicates; unknown target; batch atomicity; delta pointers; one undo step per gesture including the rename.
- **envelope**: the three ops join `test_ops.py`'s round-trip and frozen parametrize lists.
- **forge**: the blocked matrix grows the three ops (message, `monster:<id>` address, whole-batch rejection).
- **catalogs**: the detail route answers a shipped template verbatim; unknown id 404s with the code and remedy.
- **diagnostics**: `bundled_monster_collision` re-addressed to `monster:<id>`; the degrade path when the id doesn't resolve; `encounter_unknown_monster` clearing when the named template is added.
- **sidecar**: the rename note-remap through undo/redo, riding the phase 5 cascade suite's pattern.
- **round-trip and goldens**: the grown small module (one bundled bespoke template, keyed in an encounter) regenerated by its script; byte-stability and the pinned-osrlib golden discipline now cover `Adventure.monsters`.
- **frontend (vitest)**: `monster-builders` (seed block validity against the generated types, per-section patch builders computing whole templates, the auto-hit gesture clearing both ACs, clone prefills and next-free ids); the list's referenced-by counts; the collision inline-error claim; the create/clone dialog flows; the forge-mode offer-in-place routing (section entry actions, the picker footer action, and the backported import new-level mode all landing in the blocked-op dialog, and a forge detail-field commit rendering its 422 there); the resolution-panel relabel; address parsing for `monster:<id>`; type-level assertions.
- **e2e (Playwright)**: the milestone loop — create a project; clone a catalog monster from the Monsters section; rename it into a shipped-id collision and see the inline rejection, then to a free id; edit stats (AC, an attack line, morale); key it into an area encounter through the picker (ranked first as bundled); validation clean; publish. The milestone's fought half — the bespoke monster encountered and fought in osr-web — runs manually against the sibling checkout and is recorded in the PR, the established pattern.

## Definition of done

- The full gauntlet stays green on both toolchains, extended by the new suites; the typegen drift gate passes with the injection list still `[ApiError]`.
- The milestone works end to end by hand and in Playwright: a bespoke monster cloned, renamed through a collision rejection, edited, keyed into an encounter, and published — and fought in osr-web against the sibling checkout (manual, recorded in the PR).
- The collision rule is proven at both grains: the editor never commits a batch that introduces a bundled id shadowing the shipped catalog or another bundled template, and a foreign document carrying a collision opens, edits, and navigates without ever being locked by it.
- A template rename leaves no dangling in-document reference and no orphaned note, through undo and redo.
- The three ops block in forge mode with the detach offer, proven by the grown matrix — and every forge surface that presents the capability leads to that offer in place: the section's actions, the picker shortcut, a detail-field commit, and the backported import new-level mode all land in the blocked-op dialog, with no forge-mode surface preemptively hidden anywhere in the app.
- The effective-catalog contract holds end to end: an authored template ranks first in the pickers, resolves in encounters and wandering rows, satisfies `encounter_unknown_monster` the moment it lands, and rides publish into osr-web's discovery unchanged.
- The grown small module round-trips byte-identically under the pinned osrlib, so bundled-template serialization is under the golden discipline from this phase forward.
- Every phase 4 item in the spec's roadmap entry is traceable to code and tests here, or explicitly deferred above with its phase named — including the spells/classes catalog correction recorded in scope.
